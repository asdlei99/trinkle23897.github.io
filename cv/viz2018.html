Playing FPS Games With Environment-Aware Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning
Shihong Song1, # Jiayi Weng1, # Hang Su1 Dong Yan1 Jun Zhu1
1Tsinghua University      #The authors contributed equally to this work.


Abstract

First-person-shooter (FPS) games are good platforms for AI as they require fast cognition as well as fast decision making. However, solutions using end-to-end networks lack understanding of environment and ability to deal with complex circumstances. To address these issues, we propose a novel star-shaped hierarchical reinforcement learning model, which works in a manager-worker fashion incorporating environmental signals. Specifically, the manager grasps a fair understanding of environment, encodes the environmental information in a compressive representation and decides which combination of workers' actions will be used for the current step. Workers, disentangled with each other, make decision themselves by utilizing environmental information given by manager. With environmental signals appropriately harnessed, the reasonability of the decision making process is largely enhanced by our proposed hierarchical structures. Extensive experiments on FPS games show our bot significantly outperforms previous bots and won a recent international competition.
